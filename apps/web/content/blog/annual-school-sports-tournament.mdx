Running an annual school sports tournament takes planning, clear roles, and the right tools. Here’s a concise way to do it.

**Start early.** Decide the date, venue, and which sports and age groups you’ll include. Get approval from school leadership and block the calendar.

**Define categories.** Use age (e.g. U14, U17) or class (e.g. 9th, 10th). Keep categories consistent so students and parents know where they fit.

**Set deadlines.** Registration, team lists, and draws should have clear cut-offs. Publish a simple timeline so coordinators and coaches can follow it.

**Use a single place for results.** Spreadsheets and paper get messy. A platform that handles brackets, scorecards, and results in one place keeps the tournament on track and makes it easy to publish standings and winners.

**Communicate.** Share the schedule, rules, and any changes with coaches and students. A short briefing before the event reduces confusion on the day.

With clear structure and one source of truth for data, your annual tournament can run smoothly and scale as your school grows.
