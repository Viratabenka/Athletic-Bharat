Should your tournament categories be by age (U14, U17) or by class (9th, 10th)? Both work; the choice depends on your goals.

**Age-based (e.g. U14, U17).** Good when you want fair physical parity. Same age band means more even competition. You’ll need to check dates of birth and set clear cut-off dates. Common in inter-school or state-level events.

**Class-based (e.g. 9th, 10th).** Simpler for school logistics: everyone in a grade plays together. No need to verify DOB. Works well for house events or within-school tournaments where participation matters more than strict parity.

**Mixed.** Some schools run age groups for certain sports (e.g. athletics) and class groups for others (e.g. house cricket). Our platform supports both: you define categories and eligibility per competition so you can mix and match.

**Eligibility.** Whatever you choose, document it (e.g. “U14 = born on or after 1 April 2011”). Store that in the category so coordinators and coaches see the same rules. That reduces disputes and keeps the tournament fair.
